Dieter Rams

Industrial designer whose work shaped modern product language.

Dieter Rams (born 1932) is best known for his long career at Braun and for articulating a clear design ethic: make products that are understandable, unobtrusive, and built to last.

Rams distilled his thinking into “Ten Principles for Good Design”, emphasizing usefulness, honesty, and as little design as possible. The goal is not decoration — it’s clarity.
